Architecture Decisions 
In the Azure Portal, I had to make three foundational decisions. For a static website, the industry-standard recommendations are:

Performance Tier: Standard vs. Premium

My choice: Standard (General Purpose v2).

Why: Premium is optimized for low-latency data access (like disk storage for VMs). Standard is incredibly fast for web assets (HTML/CSS) and exponentially cheaper.

Redundancy: LRS vs. ZRS vs. GRS

My choice: LRS (Locally Redundant Storage).

Why: LRS keeps three copies of my data within a single data center (cheapest, high availability). ZRS replicates across three distinct data centers within the same region (highly resilient to datacenter outages). GRS (Geo-Redundant) is overkill for a basic static site and significantly increases costs.

Access Tier: Hot vs. Cool

My choice: Hot.

Why: The Hot tier has higher storage costs but lower access/read costs. Since web users will constantly be reading your HTML/CSS files, the Hot tier saves money on high-traffic sites.
